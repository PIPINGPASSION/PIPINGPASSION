P&ID Symbols

The most common P&ID symbols a piping designer encounters, with practical notes on what they mean for your routing and layout decisions. Based on ISA 5.1 and ISO 10628 conventions.

Valves

Gate Valve
Bidirectional shutoff. Used for isolation. Do not use for throttling.
Globe Valve
Throttling valve. Higher pressure drop than gate. Flow direction matters.
Ball Valve
Quarter-turn, full bore, low pressure drop. Preferred for on/off service.
Check Valve
Allows flow in one direction only. Arrow on P&ID shows flow direction.
Butterfly Valve
Wafer or lug body. Low face-to-face. High Cv. Throttling or on/off.
Control Valve
Throttles flow automatically based on process signal. Requires upstream straight run.
PSV / Safety Valve
Spring-loaded. Opens at set pressure. Must be vertical. Size outlet for reaction forces.
Needle Valve
Fine throttling for small bore instrument connections. Not for isolation.

Instruments

Pressure Indicator (PI)
Local pressure gauge. No signal output. Verify accessibility for operator.
Pressure Transmitter (PT)
Transmits pressure signal to DCS. Requires process connection with isolation valve.
Flow Indicator (FI)
Local flow indication. Various element types: orifice plate, rotameter, etc.
Temperature Element (TE)
Thermowell + sensor. Verify thermowell fits inside pipe and insertion length is correct.
Level Indicator (LI)
Local level gauge on vessel. Connections must be on vessel, not piping.
Control Valve (FCV)
Flow control valve. Tag prefix indicates variable: F=flow, P=pressure, T=temp, L=level.

Equipment

Vessel / Drum
Pressure vessel. Nozzle orientation from vendor drawing. Check bundle pull direction on exchangers.
Heat Exchanger
Shell & tube exchanger. Allow bundle pull clearance. Identify shell side vs tube side.
Centrifugal Pump
Most common rotating equipment. Suction conditions critical. Check nozzle loads vs API 610.
Compressor
High nozzle load sensitivity. API 617. Vibration isolation critical. Dynamic analysis required.
Column / Tower
Distillation column. Multiple nozzles at various elevations. Check tray numbering convention.
Filter / Strainer
Y-strainer or basket strainer. Orient drain for accessibility. Space for basket removal.

Piping Symbols

Spectacle Blind
Two-position blind — solid or open. Flipped to isolate or reinstate. Show position on P&ID.
Expansion Loop
Absorbs thermal expansion. Dimension in 3D model with insulation. Coordinate with stress engineer.
Flexible Hose
Flexible connection to absorb vibration or movement. Limited service temperature range.
Reducer (Concentric)
Symmetrical size change. Use eccentric on pump suction (flat side up to prevent gas lock).
Drain / Vent Point
Connection to drain or vent the line. Every low point needs a drain, every high point a vent.
Flange
Bolted joint for maintenance access, valve installation, or equipment connection.
Weld (Field)
Field weld — made during construction on site. Minimize field welds; maximize shop fabrication.
Weld (Shop)
Shop weld — made in fabrication shop. Lower cost, better quality control than field weld.

Note: P&ID symbols vary between companies and projects. Always read the legend sheet on day one — never assume a symbol means the same thing as your last project.
