Piping Glossary

Every term a piping designer needs to know — defined clearly, without academic padding. From NPS to weldolet, from MOC to water hammer.

A
As-Built DrawingLayout
A drawing updated after construction to reflect the actual installed condition of piping and equipment. In practice, as-builts are often incomplete or outdated — treat them as a starting hypothesis, not a confirmed fact.
ASME B31.3Codes
The primary piping code for process plants — refineries, chemical plants, petrochemical facilities. Covers design, materials, fabrication, examination, and testing of process piping.
Anchor (Support)Supports
A pipe support that restrains all six degrees of freedom — three translational and three rotational. Transfers all thermal expansion forces and moments to the structure. Design the structure to accept these loads.
API 610Standards
American Petroleum Institute standard for centrifugal pumps for petroleum, petrochemical and natural gas industries. Defines allowable nozzle loads among other requirements.
API 570Standards
Piping inspection code covering in-service inspection, rating, repair and alteration of metallic piping systems. Critical knowledge for brownfield work.
B
Battery LimitLayout
The boundary of a process unit or plant area. Piping crossing a battery limit typically requires special documentation, isolation, and often a different piping class specification.
BOM / Bill of MaterialsDocumentation
A complete list of all piping components required to fabricate a spool or construct a line. Extracted from the 3D model or isometric and used for procurement.
Branch ConnectionLayout
A connection from a smaller pipe (branch) into a larger pipe (header/run). Connection type (tee, weldolet, sockolet) depends on the branch-to-header size ratio and service requirements.
Butt Weld (BW)Welding
A weld joining two pipe ends or fittings end-to-end. The standard connection for NPS 2 and above in most process piping. Requires bevelling, alignment, and full penetration weld.
C
Caesar IISoftware
The industry-standard pipe stress analysis software by Hexagon. As a designer you do not run it yourself, but your routing decisions directly affect the results.
CUI (Corrosion Under Insulation)Materials
Corrosion occurring under thermal or personnel-protection insulation, often invisible until severe. Common on carbon steel lines cycling between wet and dry conditions. A major cause of failures in ageing plants.
Corrosion Allowance (CA)Materials
Additional wall thickness added to the pressure design wall to allow for corrosion over the design life of the plant. Typically 1.5mm to 6mm depending on fluid and material.
D
Design PressureDesign
The maximum pressure at which the piping system is designed to operate continuously. Used for wall thickness calculation, flange class selection, and pressure relief sizing. Always add margin above operating pressure.
DN (Diameter Nominal)Dimensions
The metric designation for pipe size. DN 100 = NPS 4. DN does not equal the actual OD — it is a nominal reference only. Used in European and international projects.
Drip LegLayout
A short vertical drop at a low point in a steam or gas line, fitted with a drain valve. Collects condensate or liquid and prevents water hammer. Missing drip legs are a leading cause of steam line failures.
E
Eccentric ReducerFittings
A reducer where the centreline of the two ends is offset. Used on pump suction to prevent gas pockets (flat side up) and on lines requiring drainage (flat side down). Do not substitute a concentric reducer without checking the hydraulic requirements.
EIV (Emergency Isolation Valve)Safety
A valve designed to be closed rapidly in an emergency to isolate a section of piping. Must be reachable within seconds from a safe position — this is a design requirement, not an afterthought.
F
FAT (Factory Acceptance Test)Testing
Testing performed on equipment or valves at the manufacturer's facility before shipment. A chance to catch issues before the equipment reaches site.
Flange ClassStandards
The pressure-temperature rating classification of flanges per ASME B16.5: 150, 300, 600, 900, 1500, 2500. Higher class = higher allowable pressure. Class selection depends on design pressure, temperature, and material.
Free SpanSupports
The unsupported length of pipe between two supports. Maximum free span is determined by allowable stress, pipe weight, and deflection limits. Exceeding it causes sag, vibration, and potential failure.
G
GasketComponents
A sealing element compressed between two flange faces. Single-use — never reuse a gasket. Type depends on service: spiral-wound (general service), ring-type joint RTJ (high pressure/temp), full-face (low pressure, cast iron flanges).
Guide (Support)Supports
A pipe support that restrains lateral movement but allows axial movement (thermal expansion along the pipe axis). Used in combination with anchors and stops to control thermal expansion routing.
H
Hot TapConstruction
A branch connection made to a live pipe under pressure without shutting down the system. Requires specialized equipment, hot work permits, and careful engineering. High risk operation — design the branch to make it feasible.
HydrotestTesting
Pressure test performed with water at typically 1.5× design pressure (ASME B31.3). Verifies integrity of welds and fittings before commissioning. Requires supports designed for water-filled weight.
I
Isometric DrawingDocumentation
A 3D-view drawing of a single piping line showing all dimensions, fittings, supports, weld joints, and bill of materials. The primary fabrication document for piping spools.
L
Line ListDocumentation
A project database listing every piping line with its design pressure, temperature, fluid service, material, insulation requirements, and applicable code. The data companion to the P&ID. Cross-reference everything.
LR Elbow (Long Radius)Fittings
A 90° elbow with a centreline radius of 1.5× the pipe nominal diameter. Standard for most process piping. Lower pressure drop and lower stress intensification factor than short-radius elbows. Default choice unless space is critical.
M
MOC (Management of Change)Process
Formal procedure for documenting, reviewing, and approving any change to design, materials, or operating conditions. Bypassing MOC is how small changes become expensive disasters.
MTO (Material Take-Off)Documentation
A quantified list of all piping materials required for a project or spool, used for procurement. Accuracy of MTO directly affects project cost and schedule.
N
NPS (Nominal Pipe Size)Dimensions
The imperial designation for pipe size used in North America and internationally. NPS does not equal the actual OD for small bore pipe. Example: NPS 2 has OD of 60.3mm, not 50mm.
NozzleEquipment
The connecting point between a vessel, heat exchanger, pump or other piece of equipment and the piping system. Nozzles have allowable load limits (forces and moments) that piping stress analysis must satisfy.
O
Olet (Weldolet/Sockolet/Threadolet)Fittings
A branch fitting that combines reinforcement and the branch connection in one component. Weldolet for butt-weld branches, Sockolet for socket-weld, Threadolet for threaded. Use when branch is less than 50% of header size.
P
P&ID (Piping and Instrumentation Diagram)Documentation
The primary engineering document showing all process equipment, piping, instrumentation, and control systems. The designer's bible. Verify the revision every morning before starting work.
Piping Class (Piping Spec)Standards
A project-specific document defining all allowable materials, pressure ratings, and components for a given service and pressure class. Everything you model must comply with the applicable piping class.
PMS (Piping Material Specification)Documentation
Same as Piping Class. A document specifying exactly which pipes, fittings, flanges, valves, and gaskets are permitted for each service category.
PSV (Pressure Safety Valve)Safety
A spring-loaded valve that opens automatically when process pressure exceeds the set pressure, protecting the system from overpressure. Must be mounted vertically upright. Outlet piping must be designed for reaction forces.
R
RTJ (Ring-Type Joint)Flanges
A flange facing type using a metallic ring gasket seated in a groove machined into the flange face. Used for high-pressure, high-temperature service. Higher integrity than raised-face flanges. Requires more precise alignment.
S
Schedule (Pipe)Dimensions
A wall thickness designation for pipe. Higher schedule = thicker wall = higher pressure rating and lower internal diameter. SCH 40 and SCH 80 are most common. Always verify the schedule in the piping class before routing.
SpoolFabrication
A prefabricated section of piping assembled in a shop, transported to site, and installed. Good spool design minimizes field welds, maximizes shop fabrication, and accounts for installation access.
SR Elbow (Short Radius)Fittings
A 90° elbow with centreline radius of 1.0× the nominal diameter. Saves space but has higher pressure drop and significantly higher stress intensification factor. Avoid unless space is critical and stress analysis confirms acceptability.
Stop (Support)Supports
A pipe support that restrains axial movement but allows lateral movement. Used to control thermal expansion direction. A guide without a coordinated stop is half a system.
T
Tie-InBrownfield
The connection point between new piping and existing in-service piping. Requires careful planning: isolation, drain, purge, weld procedure, inspection, and usually a shutdown window.
TQ (Technical Query)Documentation
A formal question raised to the client, process engineer, or engineering lead requesting clarification on a technical issue. Raise a TQ for every discrepancy — it creates a documented trail and protects you.
TrunnionSupports
A cylindrical attachment welded to a pipe to carry vertical loads at a support point, especially on large-bore hot lines. Avoids point loading on the pipe wall.
U
UT (Ultrasonic Testing)Inspection
Non-destructive examination method using sound waves to detect flaws in welds and measure wall thickness. Essential tool for brownfield — use a UT gauge to measure existing pipe wall thickness before tying in.
V
Valve ActuatorValves
A device for operating a valve remotely or automatically: handwheel (manual), gear operator (large valves), pneumatic actuator, electric motor operator. Specify based on torque requirement and operational urgency.
VentLayout
An opening at a high point in a piping system to release trapped gas or air during filling or draining. Every high point needs a vent. Missing vents cause air locks, incomplete draining, and hydrotest problems.
W
Water HammerOperations
A hydraulic shock wave caused by sudden changes in fluid velocity — rapid valve closure, pump trip, or steam meeting trapped condensate. Devastating forces. Prevent with proper drainage, slow-closing valves, and surge analysis on long lines.
Weld MapDocumentation
A drawing identifying all weld joints in a piping system, assigned weld numbers for tracking inspection records. Required for high-pressure and high-temperature service.
