Every year, seasoned piping designers retire — and decades of hard-earned knowledge walk out the door with them.

This book is a rescue mission.

Written by a working piping designer with seven years of field experience, it captures the collective wisdom of the senior professionals who shaped his career — veterans with twenty, thirty, and over forty years in the trade.

Their lessons, their mistakes, their war stories, and their tricks of the craft are woven together here in the voice of a single seasoned mentor, guiding the reader from first principles to advanced practice.

This is not a textbook. It is a mentorship in book form — the conversation every junior designer deserves but few ever get.

Inside you will find

  • 25 chapters covering every aspect of piping design, from P&IDs to brownfield revamps
  • 66 field-tested tips born from real projects and real mistakes
  • 14 stories from the field that no classroom will ever teach you
  • A complete guide to codes, stress analysis, valves, welding, corrosion, and plant layout
  • The most comprehensive brownfield engineering chapter available to designers today
  • A 70+ term glossary for day-one readiness
